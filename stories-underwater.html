DEATH IN THE YARD

.



Feet are sinking in mud and slime, water is practically coming into the house. The only protection for the houses where Mahmud Jamal, and his family and friends, have been living for years now, are just a few plastic bags. In the village of Middle Akilpur, the water is slowly flooding everything. Where once there were fields, there are now marshes. “ Most of us are farmers in these villages – explains Mahmud Jamal – but it has been a few years now that we cannot work the land anymore. Salt water has invaded the land, and if once we managed to have two crops a year, now we cannot have even one”. The rising sea level is one of the worst issues that Bangladesh now has to deal with. Its morphology, its climate, the fact that it is floating on an immense delta, exposed to more and more recurring and devastating typhoons, makes it the ideal victim of climatic change. But what puts the future at risk for Mahmud Jamal, and the other inhabitants of this village, is not just climate change. “Before the shipbreaking yards got close to our homes, we had minor problems– he goes on – Then they started cutting down the mangroves, which have been a barrier protecting us from the rising water since forever, and they started digging out the sand, destroying the natural barriers which were protecting our villages. And now, here we are in this situation, exposed to sea storms, we cannot work the land anymore, and our feet are perpetually underwater”.
